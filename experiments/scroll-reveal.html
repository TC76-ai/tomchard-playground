The problem

Most small business websites fail one basic test.

They don't tell you what the business actually does.

You land on the page and half a minute later you still can't tell whether you've found the right business.

A row of small thumbnails. A short paragraph about quality materials. A phone number tucked somewhere near the bottom. The business itself is properly good — thirty years of experience, excellent work, loyal customers — but none of that comes across.

That's the gap I close. The site explains what you do, who you do it for, and why someone should ring you. Nothing wasted. Nothing in the way.

How it works

A conversation first. Then a website.

Every business needs the website to do something different. The first step is working out what yours needs to do.

i

We talk.

About your business. What you sell, who buys it, how they find you — and the question that matters most: what you'd want a stranger to do after landing on your site.

ii

I sketch the site.

Just the structure on a page. What's on the homepage, what's in the gallery, what each page is supposed to achieve. You tell me where it's wrong. We adjust until it's right.

iii

I build it.

Hand-built, designed around your business specifically. Photos go where they sell the work. The phone number lives where customers can see it. Mobile-perfect from the first line of code.

iv

I hand it over.

The site goes live on your domain. Enquiry form messages come straight to your inbox. I show you how everything works. Done.
